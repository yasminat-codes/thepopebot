## Source Transcript (excerpt)

> **Host**: So you're saying most agencies are doing outbound completely wrong?
>
> **Nick**: Yeah, basically. Everyone's blasting 10,000 emails a day with these generic templates. "Hi {firstName}, I noticed your company {companyName} is doing great things in {industry}..." and it's just garbage. The reply rates are like 0.2%.
>
> **Host**: What changed for you?
>
> **Nick**: We went the opposite direction. Instead of sending 10,000 bad emails, we sent 400 really good ones per month. Each one referenced something specific — a podcast episode they were on, a blog post they wrote, a hire they just made. And our reply rate went from 0.2% to 11%.
>
> **Host**: 11%? That's insane.
>
> **Nick**: Right. And the quality of those replies was way higher too. We weren't getting "take me off your list" — we were getting "yeah, let's talk Tuesday." The math is simple: 400 emails at 11% is 44 conversations. 10,000 emails at 0.2% is 20 conversations, and most of those are angry.
>
> **Host**: So fewer emails, better targeting.
>
> **Nick**: Fewer emails, better targeting, and honestly, less work. Writing 400 personalized emails sounds like more effort but it's not — you build systems for it. Icebreaker research takes 30 seconds per lead when you have the right stack. The 10,000-email crowd is spending all their time managing bounces, warming domains, and dealing with spam complaints.

## The volume trap

The cold email world has a math problem. The conventional wisdom is simple: more emails = more replies. So agencies scale to 10,000, 20,000, 50,000 emails per month. They buy dozens of domains, run complex warming sequences, and spend half their time managing deliverability instead of actually selling.

Let's run the numbers. 10,000 emails at 0.2% gets you 20 replies. Most of those are "please remove me from your list" or worse. Maybe 5 turn into real conversations.

## What we did instead

We went the other direction. 400 emails per month, each one referencing something specific about the recipient — a podcast episode they appeared on, a blog post they published, a job posting that signals they're growing.

That specificity is the whole game. "I noticed your company is doing great things in {industry}" is a template. "Your point about churn metrics on the SaaS Breakthrough podcast was spot on" is a conversation starter.

Our reply rate: 11%. That's 44 conversations from 400 emails. And these aren't angry conversations — they're people saying "yeah, let's talk Tuesday."

## It's actually less work

Here's the part that surprises people: this approach takes less time than the volume play. Icebreaker research runs about 30 seconds per lead with a solid research stack. That's roughly 3 hours for all 400 leads.

Meanwhile, the 10,000-email operators are spending their weeks on domain rotation, bounce management, spam complaints, and sender reputation repair. They're doing more administrative work for fewer results.

## The takeaway

**Send fewer emails to people who might actually care, and say something that proves you did your homework.** That's it. That's the whole strategy.
