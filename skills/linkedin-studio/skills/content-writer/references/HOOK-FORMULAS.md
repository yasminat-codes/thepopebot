# HOOK-FORMULAS.md — LinkedIn Hook Writing Formulas

12 proven hook types. Each hook is the first 1–2 lines of a post. Rules apply to all.

---

## Universal Rules

- Max 15 words
- No AI phrases (see humanizer/references/AI-PHRASES-BLOCKLIST.md)
- Must create a curiosity gap OR a pattern interrupt
- Never start with "I" — open with the provocation, stat, or story fragment
- Hooks are judged on one question: "Does this make someone stop scrolling?"

---

## Hook Types

### 1. Question Hook
**Formula:** "Why do [specific %] of [audience] fail at [specific thing]?"
**Pattern:** Expose a failure the reader suspects is true about themselves or their peers.
**Examples:**
- "Why do 80% of founders run out of cash before product-market fit?"
- "Why can't most senior engineers pass a basic system design interview?"
- "Why do 9 out of 10 LinkedIn posts get fewer than 10 engagements?"
**Scoring criteria:** Must include specificity (number or qualifier). Generic "Why do people fail?" = reject.

---

### 2. Stat Hook
**Formula:** "[Specific number]% of [audience] still do [wrong thing]."
**Pattern:** Cite a real or credibly realistic stat that indicts common behavior.
**Examples:**
- "73% of B2B sales teams still cold call without intent data."
- "Only 6% of AI projects make it past proof of concept."
- "68% of employees say their 1:1s add zero value."
**Scoring criteria:** Stat must feel researched, not made up. Use round numbers with caution — odd numbers (73%, 61%) read as more credible.

---

### 3. Story Hook
**Formula:** "[Specific time reference], [a client/I] showed me [unexpected thing]."
**Pattern:** Drop the reader into a scene mid-action. Specific time + specific observation.
**Examples:**
- "Last Tuesday a client sent me their AI roadmap. It had 47 initiatives and zero budget owners."
- "Three weeks ago a founder called me in a panic. Their demo was in 4 hours."
- "Last month I reviewed a $2M proposal. It had one fatal flaw on page 3."
**Scoring criteria:** Must include a specific time reference and a specific detail. "One time a client showed me something interesting" = reject.

---

### 4. Bold Claim
**Formula:** "[Specific thing] is dead. Here's what replaced it."
**Pattern:** Make a declarative statement that challenges an entrenched assumption.
**Examples:**
- "The job description is dead. Here's what top candidates actually look for now."
- "Cold outreach is dead. Here's what's actually getting replies in 2026."
- "The annual review is dead. Here's what high-performing teams do instead."
**Scoring criteria:** Claim must be defensible in the post body. If you cannot back it up with evidence or a strong argument, choose a different hook type.

---

### 5. Contrarian
**Formula:** "Everyone says [conventional wisdom]. They're wrong."
**Pattern:** Directly contradict a widely held belief held by the target audience.
**Examples:**
- "Everyone says you need 10K followers before monetizing. They're wrong."
- "Everyone says show your work publicly. For technical founders, that's terrible advice."
- "Everyone says hire for culture fit. That's why your teams look the same."
**Scoring criteria:** Must name the specific belief being challenged. "Everyone says to work hard. I disagree." = reject.

---

### 6. Number List
**Formula:** "[N] things I learned after [specific concrete experience]"
**Pattern:** Specific number + specific experience. Listicle that earns its format.
**Examples:**
- "5 things I learned after reviewing 300 AI implementation proposals."
- "7 things nobody tells you before your first enterprise sales cycle."
- "4 things I'd do differently after building and killing a SaaS product."
**Scoring criteria:** Number must match actual content. Experience must be specific, not vague ("after years in the industry" = reject).

---

### 7. Personal Failure
**Formula:** "I lost [specific thing] because I didn't know [specific lesson]."
**Pattern:** Own a real mistake with concrete stakes. Specificity earns trust.
**Examples:**
- "I lost a $180K deal because I didn't know who the real decision-maker was."
- "I burned out twice because I didn't know how to say no to scope creep."
- "I wasted 14 months building a product because I didn't know how to validate demand."
**Scoring criteria:** Stakes must be concrete (dollar amount, time lost, relationship). "I made a mistake and learned from it" = reject.

---

### 8. Prediction
**Formula:** "By [specific year], [specific thing] will [specific outcome]. Here's why."
**Pattern:** Make a time-bound, defensible forecast that readers haven't heard yet.
**Examples:**
- "By 2027, 60% of software engineers will spend more time on AI wrappers than original code. Here's why."
- "By Q4 2026, most LinkedIn growth accounts will be AI-generated. Here's why that matters."
- "By 2028, the job title 'project manager' will not exist in tech companies. Here's why."
**Scoring criteria:** Year must be specific. Prediction must be directionally surprising. "AI will change everything soon" = reject.

---

### 9. Quote Hook
**Formula:** '"[Powerful quote]" — [who said it or context]. This changed how I see [topic].'
**Pattern:** Real quote from an unexpected source, followed by a personal insight bridge.
**Examples:**
- '"We hired the wrong people twice and both times it cost us the year." — This changed how I run interviews.'
- '"Your roadmap is a lie you tell investors." — A CTO said this to me at a conf. He wasn't wrong.'
- '"The goal isn't to get buy-in. It's to make the right thing the obvious thing." — Best ops advice I've received.'
**Scoring criteria:** Quote must be real or at minimum credibly attributed. Anonymous is fine. Fabricated inspirational quotes = reject.

---

### 10. Before/After
**Formula:** "[Time period] ago I was [specific situation]. Now I'm [specific contrast]."
**Pattern:** Create a vivid contrast between two concrete states.
**Examples:**
- "18 months ago I was pitching to rooms of 5 people. Now I charge $20K for the same content."
- "2 years ago I was billing by the hour. Now I price by outcome. The difference is everything."
- "6 months ago I had 340 followers. Now posts get 50K+ views. Here's what changed."
**Scoring criteria:** Both states must be specific and concrete. Time reference must be included. "Before I was bad, now I'm good" = reject.

---

### 11. Challenge
**Formula:** "Stop doing [specific thing]. Start doing [specific alternative] instead."
**Pattern:** Direct command that interrupts a behavior the audience recognizes in themselves.
**Examples:**
- "Stop writing long proposals. Start sending a one-page pre-read instead."
- "Stop scheduling discovery calls. Start sending a 3-question Loom first."
- "Stop optimizing your resume. Start building proof of work."
**Scoring criteria:** Both the thing to stop and the alternative must be specific. "Stop wasting time. Start being productive." = reject.

---

### 12. Curiosity Gap
**Formula:** "There's one thing every [audience] gets wrong about [topic]."
**Pattern:** Imply exclusive knowledge. Reader must read on to find out what it is.
**Examples:**
- "There's one thing every founder gets wrong about their first enterprise sale."
- "There's one mistake every AI consultant makes in their first client proposal."
- "There's one thing every LinkedIn creator misunderstands about the algorithm."
**Scoring criteria:** Must feel like the answer is genuinely non-obvious. If the answer is "you need to work harder," pick a different hook type.

---

## Hook Selection Guide

| Situation | Best hook types |
|---|---|
| Sharing a personal story | 3 (Story), 7 (Personal Failure), 10 (Before/After) |
| Sharing data or research | 2 (Stat), 8 (Prediction), 4 (Bold Claim) |
| Teaching a lesson | 6 (Number List), 11 (Challenge), 12 (Curiosity Gap) |
| Challenging conventional wisdom | 5 (Contrarian), 4 (Bold Claim), 1 (Question) |
| Sharing a quote or external insight | 9 (Quote), 12 (Curiosity Gap) |
