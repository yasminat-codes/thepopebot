# CTA-LIBRARY.md — Call-to-Action Template Library

40+ CTA templates organized by style. One CTA per post — never stack two.

---

## Universal Rules

- Max 20 words per CTA
- Must match post content — a CTA about "your biggest challenge" only works if the post discussed challenges
- Never use "Like and share if you found this valuable" — this is a LinkedIn cliche
- Never use "Follow me for more" as the primary CTA — it reads as begging
- Direct CTAs require a specific trigger word (e.g., "DM me 'AUDIT'") — not "DM me"
- Place CTA as the final element after a blank line separator
- Question CTAs must be genuinely answerable — not rhetorical

---

## Soft CTAs

Use when: Post is reflective, story-based, or you want to invite discussion without pushing an action.

1. "What's been your experience with [topic from post]?"
2. "Which of these resonates most with where you are right now?"
3. "Have you seen this pattern in your org?"
4. "What would you add to this list?"
5. "What's the one thing you wish someone had told you earlier about [topic]?"
6. "Curious — does this match what you're seeing in your industry?"
7. "What's the hardest part of [topic] for your team right now?"
8. "Have you navigated something similar? What worked?"
9. "Am I missing something here?"
10. "I'd love to know what you think — especially if you disagree."
11. "Which of these did you learn the hard way?"
12. "What's been your biggest unlock on this?"

## Medium CTAs

Use when: Post is educational or insight-driven and you want active engagement — comments, saves, shares.

1. "Drop your biggest [topic] challenge below."
2. "Tag someone who needs to hear this."
3. "Save this for your next [relevant situation]."
4. "Share this with your team if you're dealing with [specific problem]."
5. "If this hit home, repost it — someone in your network needs it."
6. "Drop a 1 (agree) or 2 (disagree) below."
7. "Tell me your take in the comments — especially if you've been on both sides of this."
8. "Share the one insight that would have saved you the most time."
9. "Drop your framework for [topic] in the comments."
10. "Tag the person who helped you figure this out."
11. "Save this. You'll want it when you're hiring/pitching/building [relevant context]."
12. "Share what's working for your team on [topic]."
13. "Comment with your biggest question about [topic] — I'll answer the best ones."
14. "Repost if you've lived through this."
15. "Drop a comment if you want the full [checklist / template / breakdown]."

## Direct CTAs

Use when: Post is promoting a specific offer, resource, or service. Must have a clear trigger word or link.

1. "DM me '[TRIGGER_WORD]' for [specific resource or offer]."
2. "DM me '[TRIGGER_WORD]' and I'll send you the [template / checklist / guide]."
3. "Link in comments for [specific thing — not 'more info']."
4. "Comment '[TRIGGER_WORD]' below and I'll DM you the [resource]."
5. "Book a free [30-min / 45-min] [call type] — link in bio."
6. "Download the full [guide / playbook / checklist] — link in comments."
7. "Apply for [program / cohort / workshop] — link in comments. [N] spots left."
8. "DM me '[TRIGGER_WORD]' if you want to see how I'd approach [specific problem] for your team."
9. "Comment '[TRIGGER_WORD]' and I'll share the exact process we used."
10. "Sign up for [newsletter / waitlist / beta] — link in comments."
11. "DM me '[TRIGGER_WORD]' if you're dealing with [specific situation] and want to talk through it."
12. "The [playbook / template / framework] is free. Comment '[TRIGGER_WORD]' and I'll send it."
13. "We have [N] spots open for [service / retainer / project] in [month]. DM me if you want details."
14. "Join [N]+ [audience type] who get [specific value] every [frequency] — link in comments."
15. "Watch the full breakdown — link in comments."

## CTA Matching Guide

| Post type | Recommended CTA style |
|---|---|
| Personal story or failure | Soft |
| Educational / tactical post | Medium or Soft |
| Opinion or contrarian take | Medium (invite disagreement) |
| Announcing a resource or tool | Direct |
| Promoting a service | Direct |
| Poll post | Soft (closing line only — poll is primary CTA) |
| Carousel | Medium or Direct |
| Document post caption | Direct (CTA to open doc) |

---

## CTA Anti-Patterns

Avoid these at all times:

- "Like and share if this was helpful" — signals low value
- "Follow me for daily tips on [topic]" — explicit follow-beg
- "Let me know your thoughts!" — too vague, reads as filler
- "DM me if interested" — no trigger word, too soft to convert
- "Check out my link in bio for more" — too vague, must name what's there
- Double CTA: "Comment below AND DM me AND share this" — pick one
- CTA that doesn't match post content — e.g., a post about leadership that ends with a sales pitch for a copywriting service
