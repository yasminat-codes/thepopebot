# Audience — Who Yasmine Talks To

> Canonical audience reference. Consumed by `ls:research-engine`, `ls:content-writer`, `ls:pain-point-miner`, `ls:content-calendar`.

---

## Primary ICP: Small Business Owners

**Company size:** 1-50 employees
**Revenue range:** $500K-$5M/year
**Funding:** Bootstrapped or lean-funded (no VC-backed hypergrowth)
**Industries:** E-commerce, digital marketing agencies, SaaS (pre-Series A), coaching/consulting, healthcare recruiting, B2B sales teams
**Decision-maker:** Founder, CEO, or Head of Operations

---

## Psychographics

They are:
- **Overwhelmed by AI hype.** Every vendor promises "AI-powered everything." They can not tell real from fake.
- **Tried ChatGPT with mixed results.** Used it for emails or content. Got generic output. Think that is all AI can do.
- **Skeptical of consultants.** They have been burned by people who take their money, deliver a deck, and disappear.
- **Doing too much manually.** Their team runs on spreadsheets, copy-paste workflows, and "we've always done it this way."
- **Time-poor, not dumb.** They are smart operators. They just do not have 40 hours to research AI tools.
- **Bootstrapped mindset.** Every dollar matters. They need ROI, not experiments.

## Content That Moves Them

What makes this audience stop scrolling and read:

- **Specific numbers.** "Reduced from 6 hours to 20 minutes" beats "improved efficiency"
- **Before/after.** Show the transformation, not just the result
- **Simple frameworks.** 3-step, 4-question, 2-rule — they want actionable, not academic
- **Founder stories.** Solo founder building at 2 AM resonates with people who built their businesses the hard way
- **Proof over promises.** Screenshots, metrics, specific client outcomes — not "we deliver results"
- **Honest takes.** "Most AI projects fail" gets more trust than "AI will transform your business"

What makes them scroll past:
- Corporate jargon (synergy, leverage, ecosystem)
- Vague promises ("unlock your potential")
- Emoji walls
- Generic listicles without specifics
- Guru positioning ("I'll teach you the secret")

## Secondary Audience: AI Builders / Solo Founders

Other people building AI systems, solo founders, indie hackers. They follow for:
- Technical insights (how she built the 12-agent team)
- Solo founder reality (building alone, night coding, work-life balance)
- Behind-the-scenes (what works, what broke, what she learned)

**Role:** They share, comment, and amplify. They are not buying clients — they are distribution.

## Buying Journey Map

| Stage | Mindset | Content Type | CTA Style |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Unaware** | "We're fine, just busy" | Thought leadership: challenge assumptions, bold takes, industry truths | None — just make them think |
| **Aware** | "Maybe AI could help us" | Education: frameworks, how-to guides, tool breakdowns, myth-busting | Soft — "What's been your experience?" |
| **Considering** | "Is this consultant legit?" | Social proof: case studies, before/after, client results, behind-the-scenes | Medium — "DM me 'AUDIT' for a free assessment" |
| **Ready** | "I want to talk to her" | CTA: specific offers, consult invites, limited spots, direct asks | Direct — "Book a call: [link]" |

**Key insight:** Most of Yasmine's LinkedIn audience is in Unaware or Aware. The content strategy should over-index on Thought Leadership and Education (60% combined) to move people into Considering, then use Social Proof and CTAs to convert.
