# Platform-Specific AI Patterns

**How AI writes on each platform, what's unique to each, and how to fix it.**

---

## LinkedIn — The Most AI-Contaminated Platform

LinkedIn has its own AI dialect. Experienced readers recognize it instantly. The patterns below cover every format AI defaults to on the platform.

---

### The Core LinkedIn AI Formats

AI generates LinkedIn posts in a small number of predictable templates. Recognizing the template is the first diagnostic step.

**Format 1: The Lesson List**
```
[I did/experienced X.]

Here's what I learned:

• Lesson 1
• Lesson 2
• Lesson 3

[Vague inspirational closer.]

What do you think?
```

**Format 2: The Revelation Arc**
```
[Shocking/vulnerable opener.]

[One sentence per line.]

[Building to the insight.]

[The "truth" that reframes everything.]

[CTA or engagement beg.]
```

**Format 3: The Milestone Announcement**
```
[I'm thrilled/excited/honored to announce...]

[What happened + effusive gratitude.]

[List of people to thank.]

[Moving forward, we remain committed to...]

[#hashtags #everywhere]
```

**Format 4: The Contrarian Take**
```
[Hot take / Unpopular opinion / Controversial:]

[Familiar claim everyone agrees with stated as if controversial.]

[Three reasons why.]

[But here's the thing:]

[Hedge back to center.]

[Agree or disagree?]
```

**Format 5: The Story Arc**
```
[X years ago, I was struggling with Y.]

[I tried everything. Nothing worked.]

[Then I discovered Z.]

[It changed everything.]

[Here's what I did: (numbered list)]

[Save this. Share this. Follow me.]
```

All five of these are AI formats. Real posts don't follow a formula — they have a point.

### LinkedIn Structure AI Tells

| AI Structure | Human Fix |
|-------------|-----------|
| Every line is one sentence with a line break | Write 2–4 sentences per paragraph |
| Post opens with "I" + past tense revelation | Open with the interesting observation or outcome |
| Emoji at start of every bullet | Remove emojis from bullets, or keep one max |
| Post ends with 3 questions | End with one strong statement or one specific question |
| "3 lessons from X" / "5 things about Y" format | Write the argument in prose |
| 8-12 bullet points, each one line | Cut to 3–4 max, expand each with context |
| Every paragraph is 1-2 lines | Write real paragraphs where ideas connect |
| Numbered list of "steps" with no depth | Either commit to real depth or cut the list |
| CTA in every post | Reserve CTAs for when they're genuinely relevant |
| Post ends with hashtag block: `#Leadership #Growth #Innovation` | One or two specific tags max, or none |
| Excessive whitespace (every line a paragraph) | Varies — some LinkedIn readers prefer white space, but don't use it as a crutch |
| Ends with "What's your experience with X?" | Ask the question you actually want answered, or just end |
| "I'll start: [answer]" | Obvious engagement farming — cut |

### LinkedIn Post Types — AI Tells by Format

**Milestone posts (new job, promotion, launch):**
- AI: "I'm thrilled to announce that after an incredible journey..." → Human: announce the thing in one sentence, say something specific about it
- AI: gratitude list (team, mentors, family, everyone who believed in me) → Human: thank one specific person and say exactly why
- AI: "Moving forward, I'm excited to..." → Human: say what's actually next or just end

**Opinion posts:**
- AI: labels opinion before stating it ("Unpopular opinion:", "Hot take:") → Human: just state the opinion
- AI: immediately hedges after the "controversial" claim → Human: commit to the position
- AI: ends with "Agree or disagree?" → Human: ends with the point

**Career/advice posts:**
- AI: "X years in [industry] taught me..." → Human: skip the credential, make the point
- AI: lessons formatted as bullets → Human: write the lessons as argument, not inventory
- AI: every lesson is a vague virtue ("Be authentic", "Work hard", "Stay curious") → Human: specific and counterintuitive

**Personal story posts:**
- AI: "I failed. Here's what happened." → Human: skip the meta-announcement, just tell the story
- AI: story ends with "what I learned" bullet list → Human: the ending IS the lesson, don't list it
- AI: "I was at my lowest when..." → Human: describe what actually happened — show, don't narrate

---

### What Human LinkedIn Sounds Like

- Opens with the observation or outcome, not the setup
- Has a specific, defensible point of view — not "here are three things"
- Uses specific details: numbers, names, dates, places, actual costs
- Doesn't list lessons: makes the argument
- Ends with something real — a conclusion, a question you actually care about, or just the point
- Doesn't beg for engagement
- Sounds like it came from a specific person — has texture, idiosyncrasy, a specific context
- Occasionally disagrees with something — has friction

## Email — Professional Register Traps

AI treats every email like a formal letter. It front-loads pleasantries, over-explains context the recipient already has, and closes with elaborate sign-offs. Most AI email reads like it was written by someone who learned email from a 1998 business writing textbook.

### Email Tone by Context

| Email type | What AI does wrong | What humans do |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------|
| Quick internal update | Writes a formal letter | 2-3 sentences, no opener, no closer |
| Client project update | Over-explains everything | Short, specific, flags only what changed |
| Follow-up | Restates the entire previous conversation | One line: what you're following up on + what you need |

## Twitter/X — Voice and Compression

AI tweets read like someone trying to sound smart on Twitter without understanding how Twitter works. The giveaways are structure, labeling, and over-explanation.

### Twitter/X Formats — What AI Gets Wrong

**The Opinion Tweet:**
- AI: "Hot take: [moderate claim that everyone agrees with]. This is why X matters more than ever."
- Human: "[Specific, defensible claim without the label]. [One supporting line or just the claim alone.]"

**The Thread:**
- AI: Setup tweet → connect-the-dots tweets → summary tweet → engagement ask
- Human: Start with the most interesting point → add depth → end when done. No summary. No ask.

**The Reaction Tweet:**
- AI: "This. This is everything. We need more conversations like this." + quote tweet
- Human: Quote tweet + one specific sentence about why it matters or what's missing

**The Advice Tweet:**
- AI: "PSA: You don't need to [X] to [Y]. [Three bullets of obvious advice.]"
- Human: "[Counterintuitive specific claim about X.] [Why, in one sentence or a brief story.]"

**The Hot Take:**
- AI: Labels it as a hot take. The claim is usually not actually controversial. Immediately hedges in the next tweet.
- Human: Just makes the claim. Stands behind it. Doesn't label it.

**The Personal Update:**
- AI: "Excited to share that I've [done X]! [Gratitude list.] [What this means for my journey.]"
- Human: "[What happened.] [One specific thing about it that's interesting or honest.]"

---

### What Good Twitter Sounds Like

- First tweet is the most interesting point — not setup, not context
- Reads like a specific person with a specific voice
- Occasional lowercase, fragment, comma splice — informal register
- Doesn't explain the joke
- Doesn't beg for shares or follows
- Has a take — not just information
- When wrong, admits it specifically — not with "I was humbled to learn"
- Short when the point is short; long when the point needs length

## Reports and Formal Documents

Even formal documents have AI tells:

| Pattern | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Every section ends with a recap paragraph | Cut — reader just read it |
| Executive summary longer than 1 page | Key decisions only |
| `In conclusion, this report has demonstrated` | End with a recommendation, not a recap |
| Every heading is a 6+ word noun phrase | Shorter, direct headings |
| "It is recommended that" for every recommendation | "Recommendation: [action verb]" |
| Passive voice throughout | Active — say who does what |
| "Further investigation may be warranted" | "Recommend investigating X by [date]" |
| Every recommendation uses "should", "could", or "may" | Use "will" and "recommend" — commit |
| `Key findings include the following:` + bullets | Just list the findings without the preamble |
| Vague next steps with no owner and no date | "[Person] to [action] by [date]" |
