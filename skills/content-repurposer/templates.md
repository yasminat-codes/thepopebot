# Content Repurposer — Templates

## Tweet Thread Template

```
[TWEET 1 — THE HOOK]
Bold claim, surprising stat, or counterintuitive take. No "I just learned..." preamble.
Must make someone stop scrolling.

[TWEET 2-4 — KEY INSIGHTS]
One insight per tweet. Use concrete details:
- Numbers over adjectives ("3x faster" not "much faster")
- Specifics over generalities ("we emailed 400 leads" not "we did outreach")
- Stories over statements ("Last Tuesday, a client called us panicking..." not "Clients often struggle with...")

[TWEET 5-8 — SUPPORTING EVIDENCE / STORY]
Expand on the most interesting point. This is where you can:
- Share a mini-story from the transcript
- Drop a direct quote (use quotation marks)
- Show a before/after comparison

[TWEET 9-10 — WRAP-UP]
Restate the core idea in one sentence.
Soft CTA: "If this resonates, I write about [topic] a lot" or similar.
No "Follow me for more" or "Retweet if you agree".
```

### Tweet rules
- Each tweet is independent. Someone reading tweet 5 alone should still get value.
- No hashtags anywhere.
- No "1/", "2/", or "Thread:" labels.
- Under 280 characters per tweet. If it's over, cut — don't compress with abbreviations.
- Line breaks between logical chunks within a tweet are fine.
- Quotes from the transcript use quotation marks and attribution.

## LinkedIn Post Template

```
[HOOK — FIRST LINE]
This is the only line visible before "see more". It must earn the click.
Patterns that work:
- Counterintuitive statement: "The best salespeople never sell."
- Specific result: "We went from 2 to 47 clients in 90 days."
- Provocative question: "Why do 90% of cold emails get ignored?"

[BODY — 3-5 SHORT PARAGRAPHS]
Each paragraph is 1-2 sentences max. White space is your friend.

Paragraph 1: Set up the problem or context (1-2 sentences).
Paragraph 2: The key insight or turning point (1-2 sentences).
Paragraph 3: What changed / the result (1-2 sentences).
Paragraph 4 (optional): A second supporting insight.
Paragraph 5: Takeaway — what should the reader do differently?

[CLOSING QUESTION]
End with a genuine question that invites comments.
Not "What do you think?" — too generic.
Instead: "What's the one thing you'd change about your onboarding process?"

[HASHTAGS]
(blank line)
#relevanthashtag #anotherone #maxfive
```

### LinkedIn rules
- 150-300 words total (not counting hashtags).
- No emojis as bullet points. If you use emojis at all, max 2 in the entire post.
- No "I'm excited to announce..." or "Thrilled to share..." openings.
- No tagging people who weren't in the transcript.
- 3-5 hashtags at the end only. Never inline.
- Write in first person.

## Newsletter Draft Template

```
Subject: [COMPELLING SUBJECT LINE]
Preview: [1-SENTENCE PREVIEW TEXT — what shows in inbox before opening]

---

[OPENING — STORY OR SCENARIO]
Start with a concrete moment. Not "In this issue, we'll cover..."
Instead: "Last week, I was on a call with a founder who told me..."
Or: "Picture this: it's 11pm, your deploy just failed, and..."
2-3 sentences that pull the reader in.

## [SECTION 1 HEADER — THE SETUP]
Context and background. What's the problem or situation?
2-3 paragraphs, each 2-4 sentences.

## [SECTION 2 HEADER — THE INSIGHT]
The core idea from the transcript. This is the meat.
Use direct quotes where they're punchy.
2-3 paragraphs.

## [SECTION 3 HEADER — THE APPLICATION]
So what? What should the reader do with this information?
Concrete, actionable. "Try this: [specific thing]" is better than
"Consider implementing a strategy for..."
1-2 paragraphs.

## The Takeaway
One sentence. The single thing you want them to remember.
Bold it.

---

[SIGN-OFF]
Short personal note or callback to the opening story.
Name.
```

### Newsletter rules
- Subject line: 6-10 words. Specific beats clever. No clickbait.
- Preview text: Complements (not repeats) the subject line.
- 500-800 words total.
- Headers use sentence case, not Title Case.
- Direct quotes from the transcript are attributed.
- One CTA max (and it's the takeaway, not a "buy my thing" pitch).
- No "If you enjoyed this, forward to a friend" boilerplate.
