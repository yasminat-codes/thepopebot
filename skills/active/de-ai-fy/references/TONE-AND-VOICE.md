# Tone and Voice — Stripping AI Tone

**How AI tone manifests, why it rings hollow, and how to inject genuine voice.**

---

## What Makes AI Tone Detectable

AI text sounds authoritative but says nothing specific. It's perpetually balanced, perpetually polished, perpetually non-committal. The tone is designed to not offend, not be wrong, and not actually take a position. Real human writing has:

- A perspective that isn't perfectly balanced
- Specific details instead of vague generalities
- The occasional strong statement, not every claim hedged
- Personality quirks — some things the writer cares about, others they don't
- Register variation — slightly different depending on context

AI text sounds like it was written by a committee trying to satisfy everyone.

---

## Tone Problem 1: The Hedge Machine

**What AI does:**
Qualifies every claim into meaninglessness.

| Hedged AI phrase | Human replacement |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| "It's important to note that..." | Just say the thing |
| "It's worth considering..." | "Consider..." or just say it |
| "One might argue that..." | "The argument is..." or make the argument |
| "It could be suggested that..." | Say what you think |
| "There may be some value in..." | "There's value in..." |
| "In certain circumstances..." | Be specific about which circumstances |
| "This tends to be the case..." | "This is the case..." or qualify specifically |
| "To some extent..." | Say how much, or cut the qualifier |
| "Generally speaking..." | Cut — then say the general thing |
| "For the most part..." | Cut, or "mostly" |
| "It appears that..." | "It is..." or "It looks like..." |
| "There is evidence to suggest..." | "Evidence shows..." or cite it |
| "Research indicates that..." | "Research shows..." or name the research |
| "Studies suggest..." | Which studies? Or cut to the finding |

**The rule:** If you can remove the hedge and the sentence is still accurate, remove it.

## Tone Problem 2: Performative Balance

**What AI does:**
Presents "both sides" even when one side is clearly stronger. Treats everything as a nuanced debate. Never commits to a view.

**Example of AI balance-washing:**
> "While some argue that remote work increases productivity, others contend that in-person collaboration fosters better teamwork. Both perspectives have merit, and the right approach may depend on the specific needs of each organization."

**Human version:**
> "Remote work tends to boost individual productivity. The trade-off is spontaneous collaboration — the kind that happens in hallways, not Zoom calls. Whether that trade-off is worth it depends entirely on what your team actually does."

**The fix:**
Have an opinion. It can be qualified, but it should be yours. The human version takes a view and explains the caveat specifically.

## Tone Problem 3: The Hollow Compliment

**What AI does:**
Opens responses with flattery. Ends responses with hollow well-wishing. Fills the text with meta-commentary about the quality of the conversation.

| Phrase | Fix |
|--------|-----|
| "Great question!" | Cut — start with the answer |
| "That's an excellent point." | Cut — respond to the point |
| "What a thoughtful observation!" | Cut |
| "You've raised some really important considerations." | Cut |
| "I hope this helps clarify things!" | Cut or "Let me know if you need more" |
| "Feel free to reach out if you have more questions!" | Cut or make it specific |
| "I look forward to hearing your thoughts!" | Cut |
| "Thanks so much for sharing this!" | Cut unless genuinely in a context where thanks apply |

## Tone Problem 4: The Empathy Performance

**What AI does:**
Performs empathy in a detached, scripted way. "I understand how frustrating this must be." "It sounds like you're going through a challenging time." This sounds like a customer service script, not a real person.

**The fix:**
In contexts that call for empathy — address the actual problem. Fewer words of empathy, more action.

**Before:**
> "I completely understand how frustrating this situation must be for you. It's understandable to feel overwhelmed when facing such challenges. I'm here to help and support you through this process."

**After:**
> "This sounds genuinely difficult. Here's what I'd do..."

## Tone Problem 5: Corporate Register in Casual Contexts

**What AI does:**
Defaults to formal corporate register even in casual contexts. "Please be advised that..." in a Slack message. "I wanted to reach out to..." in a quick email.

**Register calibration guide:**

| Context | Register |
|---------|---------|
| Slack message | Casual. Contractions, short sentences, direct |
| Internal team email | Conversational-professional |
| Client email | Professional but not stiff |
| Blog post | Conversational-authoritative |
| Social media | Casual-confident |
| Press release | Formal (one of the few cases) |
| Documentation | Clear, neutral, precise |
| Report | Professional-objective |
| Chat response | Match the user's register |

## Tone Problem 6: The Setup-Before-Every-Point

**What AI does:**
Before making any point, it telegraphs that it's about to make a point.

| Setup phrase | Fix |
|-------------|-----|
| "It's important to understand that..." | Say the thing |
| "Let me explain why this matters..." | Explain why it matters — skip the announcement |
| "Before we dive in, it's worth noting..." | Note it — then dive |
| "There are a few things to keep in mind..." | State the things |
| "Let's take a closer look at..." | Look closer — skip the announcement |
| "Now, let's explore..." | Explore — skip "let's explore" |
| "With that in mind..." | Cut |
| "That being said..." | Cut |
| "Having said that..." | Cut |
| "At this juncture..." | Cut |
| "Without further ado..." | Cut — there was no ado |

## Tone Problem 7: Vague Generalities Instead of Specifics

**What AI does:**
Makes general claims where a human would use specific details.

| Vague (AI) | Specific (human) |
|-----------|-----------------|
| "Many studies have shown..." | Which studies? By whom? When? |
| "Experts agree that..." | Which experts? In what field? |
| "Research suggests..." | What research? What does it say precisely? |
| "Companies like X have found..." | What did they actually find? |
| "There are numerous examples..." | Give one good one |
| "In recent years..." | What year? What happened? |
| "A significant number of people..." | What number? |
| "Various factors contribute..." | Which factors specifically? |

**The fix:** Either name the specific or cut the claim.

---

## Tone Problem 8: The Everything-Is-Complex Escape

**What AI does:**
When it can't or won't take a strong position, AI retreats to "this is a complex topic with many nuances."

| Escape phrase | Fix |
|--------------|-----|
| "This is a complex topic with many nuances..." | Address one nuance specifically |
| "There are many perspectives on this issue..." | Name two and say which you find more convincing |
| "It's not a simple black-and-white situation..." | OK, what is the actual situation? |
| "Every situation is different..." | What are the key variables? |
| "It depends on many factors..." | Name the most important ones |
| "The answer isn't straightforward..." | Then give the complicated-but-honest answer |

---

## Tone Problem 9: Overly Formal Closing Lines

**What AI does:**
Ends with elaborate, formal closings that no human would write organically.

| AI closing | Human version |
|-----------|--------------|
| "I trust this response has been helpful in addressing your query." | Cut entirely |
| "Should you require any further assistance, please do not hesitate to contact me." | "Let me know if you need more." |
| "Thank you for your time and consideration of this matter." | "Thanks." or cut |
| "I remain at your disposal for any further inquiries." | Cut |
| "It has been a pleasure assisting you today." | Cut |

## Voice Authenticity Markers

After tone detox, inject these human voice markers:

**Directness:**
- State the conclusion first, then explain
- Use "I think" and "I'd" when giving opinions
- Say "no" cleanly when no is the answer

**Specificity:**
- Real numbers over vague quantities
- Named examples over "companies like..."
- Specific time over "recently"

**Personality:**
- The writer has an opinion — it shows
- Some things get more attention than others (the writer cares)
- Occasional informality even in professional writing

**Imperfection:**
- Not every point needs equal development
- Some ideas are introduced and not fully resolved
- Occasional repetition for emphasis

---

## The Tone Checklist

- [ ] No hedge phrases before claims that need no hedging
- [ ] Clear perspective stated — not just "both sides"
- [ ] No meta-commentary ("great question", "I hope this helps")
- [ ] No empathy scripts — address the actual problem
- [ ] Register matches context (casual where casual, formal where formal)
- [ ] No "it's important to note" setups
- [ ] Specific details instead of vague generalities
- [ ] No "this is complex" escape hatches without follow-through
- [ ] Closing lines are short or cut
- [ ] Voice sounds like a person, not a committee
